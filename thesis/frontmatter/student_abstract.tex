\begin{abstract}
    Spreadsheet programs are one of the most widely used computational tools. Previous work identified that existing spreadsheet tools offer limited support for understanding formula logic and tracing dependencies. In particular, users spend a lot of time on information detours. This thesis presents a flow graph based visualization, which visualizes the formula dependencies as an interactive graph alongside the spreadsheet. By having the spreadsheet and the flow graph visualization side by side it aims to preserve the spatial context of the user in the spreadsheet while allowing the user to trace the dependencies of the formula even across different worksheets. To evaluate the usefulness, an explorative study was conducted with 5 participants, who had to solve three tasks and fill out the NASA-TLX, UEQ+ and answer three open-ended questions. The overall NASA-TLX mean was 27.7 out of 100 and the UEQ+ score indicates a positive user experience. In the open-ended questions the participants confirmed that they found the tool helpful to understand formulas and intuitive to use for navigating. However, the study was explorative and did not include sessions where particpants used only traditional spreadsheet tools. Therefore, to be able to correctly identify the benefits of the tool, future work should conduct a more extensive study.
\end{abstract}
