\section{Motivation}
Spreadsheets are one of the most common computer tools, with an estimated 1 billion user base \cite{gislason_excel_2021}. This popularity is largely attributed to their accessibility. Users can store and organize their data in near instant time without writing traditional code. This allows for quick "what-if" projections of their data for decision-making. \cite{zynda_first_2013}.
Even though spreadsheets do not require writing code, users still have to write formulas and possibly use multiple spreadsheet at the same time. This hidden complexity can lead to weak points in the spreadsheets. Such possible weak points are called spreadsheet smells. Common examples are Feature Envy, where a formula accumulates excessive dependencies, and Inappropriate Intimacy, when data is tightly coupled across different Spreadsheets \cite{hermans_detecting_2012}.
These smells hinder comprehension and make it harder for the user to find the relevant information. Readers of spreadsheet spend approximately 40\% of their time "seeking information".  This involves locating items, understanding interactions, tracing formula logic or understanding familiar spreadsheet concepts \cite{srinivasa_ragavan_spreadsheet_2021}. This shows that it exists a need for tools to help spreadsheet users navigating and understanding the underlying complexity of Spreadsheets.
